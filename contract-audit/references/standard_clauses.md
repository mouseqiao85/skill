# Standard Contract Clauses Reference

## Essential Clauses Checklist

Every well-structured contract should contain these fundamental elements:

### 1. Parties Identification
- Full legal names and addresses of all contracting parties
- Entity type (individual, corporation, partnership, etc.)
- Authority to enter into contract

### 2. Recitals (Background)
- Statement of purpose and context
- Recitals beginning with "WHEREAS"
- Clear statement of each party's intentions

### 3. Definitions Section
- Clear and unambiguous definitions of key terms
- Consistent use of defined terms throughout
- Avoidance of circular definitions

### 4. Scope of Work/Deliverables
- Specific, measurable deliverables
- Performance standards and acceptance criteria
- Timeline and milestones

### 5. Payment Terms
- Amounts and payment schedule
- Method of payment
- Conditions precedent to payment
- Late payment penalties

### 6. Term and Termination
- Commencement and expiration dates
- Renewal conditions
- Termination rights and procedures
- Effect of termination on obligations

### 7. Confidentiality
- Definition of confidential information
- Obligations of receiving party
- Exceptions to confidentiality
- Duration of confidentiality obligations

### 8. Intellectual Property
- Ownership of pre-existing IP
- Ownership of work product
- License grants
- Protection obligations

### 9. Representations and Warranties
- Authority to enter contract
- Accuracy of stated facts
- Compliance with laws
- Non-infringement

### 10. Liability and Indemnification
- Limitation of liability
- Mutual indemnification provisions
- Exceptions to indemnification
- Insurance requirements

### 11. Dispute Resolution
- Governing law
- Jurisdiction and venue
- Mediation/arbitration procedures
- Attorney fees allocation

## Risky Clause Templates to Avoid or Modify

### Unilateral Modification Rights
**Problematic Language:**
> "Company may modify this Agreement at any time by providing notice to Customer."

**Better Alternative:**
> "This Agreement may only be modified by written agreement signed by both parties."

### Unlimited Liability
**Problematic Language:**
> "Party A shall not be liable for any indirect, consequential, or punitive damages."

**Better Alternative:**
> "Each party's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the amount paid under this Agreement in the 12 months preceding the claim."

### Automatic Renewal
**Problematic Language:**
> "This Agreement will automatically renew for successive one-year periods."

**Better Alternative:**
> "This Agreement will terminate upon expiration of the initial term unless extended by mutual written consent."

### Indefinite Termination
**Problematic Language:**
> "Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time, for any reason."

**Better Alternative:**
> "Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon 30 days written notice."

## Industry-Specific Clauses

### Technology Services
- Service level agreements (SLAs)
- Data security and privacy provisions
- System uptime guarantees
- Data backup and recovery

### Employment Agreements
- Non-compete restrictions (geographic/time limitations)
- Non-solicitation of employees/customers
- Invention assignment clauses
- At-will employment statements

### Manufacturing/Supply
- Quality standards and inspection rights
- Force majeure provisions
- Delivery and shipping terms
- Title transfer provisions

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Legal Red Flags
- Ambiguous or undefined terms
- Contradictory provisions
- Unenforceable penalty clauses
- Violations of applicable law

### Business Red Flags
- Imbalanced risk allocation
- One-sided termination rights
- Excessive performance guarantees
- Uncapped liability exposure

### Practical Red Flags
- Unrealistic timelines
- Undefined performance standards
- Insufficient termination protections
- Poor dispute resolution mechanisms

## Best Practices for Risk Mitigation

1. **Always include a force majeure clause** to address unforeseeable circumstances
2. **Define key terms clearly** to avoid ambiguity
3. **Include integration clauses** stating the contract represents the complete agreement
4. **Ensure compliance with applicable laws** and regulations
5. **Consider insurance requirements** for high-risk activities
6. **Include appropriate limitation periods** for bringing claims
7. **Specify dispute resolution procedures** early in the contract
8. **Maintain consistent terminology** throughout the document
